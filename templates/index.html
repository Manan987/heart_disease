Understanding Heart Disease

Heart disease remains the leading cause of death globally. Understanding it is crucial for prevention.

What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease refers to several conditions affecting heart function and blood vessels. The most common is coronary artery disease.

Risk Factors

Key risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity, and family history.

Prevention

Lifestyle changes, regular exercise, healthy diet, and regular check-ups can help prevent heart disease.

Common Symptoms

Recognize these warning signs and seek medical attention if you experience them.

Chest Pain

Feeling of pressure, squeezing, or fullness in the chest. May spread to arms, neck, jaw, or back.

Prevention Steps

Take these steps to reduce your risk of heart disease.

Regular Exercise

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity weekly.
